## WCC Personal Statement

I first heard about the club from a friend on the same floor. Later, when I went to the club fair, the simple display of a wood table, two people in suits, and a huge crowd around them caught my eye. Their spiel intrigued me, and they directed me to the WCC website. 

I had heard before that being a consultant requires a diverse array of skills, but the payoffs can be enormous; in fact, my mom always used to say that she'd like to be a consultant because of the success she could accrue but couldn't because she had to take care of me and my brother; now, she says she will start doing it once me and my brother are in college. 

I researched consulting a bit more and found out the readings and resources on the website. I was surprised to find out that I had read some of the books; in particular,

- *How to Win Friends and Influence People*, Dale Carnegie
- *Good to Great,* Jim Collins
- *Secrets of Mental Math*, Arthur C. Benjamin (this one I was pretty surprised by; I wonder how you can apply this to consulting?)

I have three characteristics suited towards being in a consulting firm, but they're not what you think:

- I am lazy, which means I will strive to find the method that will complete a task with the highest quality in the shortest time
- I am an artist; I've been playing music for around ten years, which has allowed me to grow out the right side of my brain and allows me to come up with more creative solutions than what most people could think of
- I am independent; I can work well with a team if I need to, but if I have to do a task without the help of the team, I will. It's easy being a team player, but it's harder being by yourself.

In conclusion, I am an excellent fit for this esteemed organization, and hope to join your ranks soon enough. Thank you. 

Yours, Svadrut Kukunooru
